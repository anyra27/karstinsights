Methodology and provenance.

Instrument
LCAP community input survey, six priority ratings (1–5) plus six free-response prompts. Sentence-completion stem “A district that's serving every student well would…” shared across roles. Six numbered sections, ~6 minutes to complete. Live at demo.karst.build/survey.
Period
Approximately eight days in April 2026.
Sample
n = 524 anonymous responses. Role mix: 295 parents, 191 students, 35 staff, 3 leadership. Per-school n ranges from 3 (Site F) to 121 (Site H). Site-level means shown only at n ≥ 60.
Privacy
No PII collected. A per-session UUID is recorded for de-duplication only and is not personally identifying. The dataset is retained on a single Google Sheet owned by the district.
Theme synthesis
Open-text themes are computed by hand-coded keyword/regex bundles applied per respondent across the source columns for each section. A respondent may match more than one theme. Counts in this report are unique-respondent matches; percentages are over the count of respondents who answered at least one open-text question for that section. Verbatim quotes are reproduced as written; light typo cleanup only, no semantic edits.
Audience
This artifact is the input the executive team uses for next steps (LCAP narrative goals + action plan draft, May–June 2026). Mean ratings and top-three picks anchor the rank order of the six priorities. Theme blocks anchor the language used in the LCAP narrative and the family-engagement actions.
Out of scope here
Per-school individual briefs (low-n at two sites makes site comparison misleading). Public board version (different framing — single finding, single number, single repeatable sentence). Both can follow if leadership wants them.
Provenance
Source · Karst LCAP instrument · April 2026. Not for public distribution until reviewed by district leadership.

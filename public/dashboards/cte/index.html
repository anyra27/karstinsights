Other contributing factors

Two structural pressures sitting under the gap.

Beyond the timing of when students take which course, two patterns systematically lower completion: spreading across multiple pathways without committing to one, and pathways where the floor of academic failure is high enough to gate progression.

Methodology and provenance.

Cohort
A demo cohort representing the Class of 2025 across four high schools. Numbers are synthetic, derived from a de-identified district structure but perturbed so aggregates do not reflect any real district's outcomes.
Source records
De-identified district student records: pathway enrollment, course-level transcript data with academic year and pass/fail, and program flags (AVID, EL, SpEd). Original student IDs and names are not shipped with this dashboard.
Concentrator
A student who took and passed a Level 2 course in a CTE pathway.
Completer
A student who reached Level 3 capstone in a pathway and is marked completer = "Yes" in district records.
Retention rate
Completers divided by concentrators. Cells with fewer than 5 concentrators are not colored to avoid drawing conclusions from small numbers.
Consecutive vs. gap
A student is "consecutive" if their first Level 3 year is within one academic year of their first Level 2 year in the same pathway, and "gap" if it is two or more years later.
Skipped grade
For students who never reached capstone in any pathway, the grade level immediately following their first concentrator. The most common skipped grade is the place where the pathway most often ends.
Privacy
Every record on this page is synthetic demonstration data, generated by a script. No real student records were used. In a district deployment, anonymization runs offline before any data file is produced, and names and original IDs never reach the page.
Refresh
This page reads one data file at load time. In a district deployment, regenerating that file from the district's own records is the only step needed to refresh the entire dashboard.
